# 01 — What Is Artificial Intelligence?

**Audience:** complete beginners. **Time:** ~8 minutes.

## In one sentence

Artificial intelligence (AI) is the field of building computer systems that can
perform tasks which normally require human intelligence — like understanding
language, recognizing images, or making decisions.

## Two broad kinds

- **Narrow AI** does one specific thing well (spam filters, recommendation
  systems, voice assistants). Almost everything you use today is narrow AI.
- **General AI** would match humans across *all* cognitive tasks. It does not
  exist yet and remains an active research goal.

## How it actually works (the short version)

Most modern AI is built on **machine learning**: instead of writing explicit
rules, we feed a model many examples and let it learn patterns from data.

```
data  ──►  training algorithm  ──►  model (learned patterns)
                                       │
                            new input ─┘──► prediction
```

A model is not "thinking" — it is statistically mapping inputs to outputs based
on what it saw during training.

## Common misconceptions

- *"AI understands meaning."* Not really — it predicts plausible next steps from
  patterns in data.
- *"AI is always objective."* It reflects the data and choices that shaped it,
  including biases.
- *"AI knows when it is wrong."* Often it does not; confidence and correctness
  are different things.

## Key takeaway

AI is a powerful pattern-matching tool, not a mind. Understanding its limits is
as important as understanding its capabilities.
