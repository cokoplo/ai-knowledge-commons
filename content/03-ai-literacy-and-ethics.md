# 03 — AI Literacy and Ethics

**Audience:** anyone who uses or is affected by AI. **Time:** ~10 minutes.

## What is AI literacy?

AI literacy is the ability to *understand, evaluate, and use* AI tools
critically and responsibly. It is becoming a basic civic skill, like reading or
numeracy.

## Four components

1. **Awareness** — knowing when and where AI is involved in a system.
2. **Understanding** — grasping roughly how it works and where it fails.
3. **Evaluation** — judging output quality, bias, and reliability.
4. **Use** — applying tools effectively and safely.

## Key ethical questions

- **Bias & fairness:** models can reflect societal biases present in training
  data. Who is harmed, and who benefits?
- **Privacy:** what data is collected, and how is it used?
- **Transparency:** are decisions explainable and contestable?
- **Accountability:** when an AI system causes harm, who is responsible?

## A simple checklist before trusting AI output

- [ ] Did I verify the claim against a reliable source?
- [ ] Could bias have influenced this result?
- [ ] Is any personal or sensitive data exposed?
- [ ] Do I understand the limits of this tool?

## Key takeaway

AI literacy is not about coding — it is about asking better questions and
staying accountable for how these tools are used.
