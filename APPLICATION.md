## 3. Long description (the "few hundred words" field)

AI Knowledge Commons is an independent, non-commercial **public-interest
technology** project building an AI-driven personal knowledge operating system.
Modern AI work leaves useful traces across chat logs, coding agents, IDEs, issue
trackers, documentation, bookmarks, and personal note systems. Today those
records are scattered across vendors and formats, making it hard for people to
search their own history, reuse prior context, or understand how a decision was
made.

Our purpose is to help people own, understand, and reuse their own knowledge. We
are developing open educational materials, shared vocabulary, and early
user-controlled workflows for unifying chat logs, developer-tool context, and
second-brain notes into reviewable knowledge archives. The long-term vision is a
personal knowledge OS that can produce context packs, timelines, glossaries,
retrieval indexes, and summaries for the AI tools a person chooses to use.

We ground the work in **human-centered** and **privacy-preserving** values. A
personal AI memory layer should not become another opaque vendor silo. It should
make provenance visible, keep imports and exports reversible, support local-first
processing where practical, and require explicit review before sensitive context
is shared with an AI model.

The project is transparent and open by design: it lives in a public repository
where educational and product materials are released under open licenses, and
our small learning utilities are dependency-free so contributors can run them
without installing a large stack. Current materials include AI-literacy
explainers, responsible-use guidance, a public glossary, and the first
specification notes for the personal knowledge OS direction.

We do **not** sell commercial products or services, we do **not** run ads, and
the project itself does **not** collect personal data from readers or users. Any
future prototypes should be designed around user-owned archives and open export
formats. Any funds received are used solely to cover the production, hosting,
documentation, and prototype costs of these free public-interest materials.

Our mission is educational and civic: to make personal AI memory understandable,
portable, and controlled by the people who create it. This aligns directly with
public-interest technology, digital rights, responsible AI, and practical
education.

## 4. What we do (activity / purpose field)

- Publish open materials on personal knowledge OS design, AI memory, and
  user-controlled context workflows.
- Define practical patterns for unifying chat logs, developer-tool output, and
  second-brain notes.
- Maintain plain-language AI-literacy explainers and a public glossary so users
  understand the systems they rely on.
- Provide small, dependency-free utilities that support self-guided study and
  reproducible documentation.
- Advance **user ownership of AI context and memory** as a public-interest
  technology goal.

---

## 5. Alignment with Superbloom's mission

Superbloom supports work that "advances digital rights and responsible
technology … spanning research, policy, **education** and practice that shifts
how technology operates in the public interest." AI Knowledge Commons does this
by helping people understand and control the AI memory layer forming around
their work. The project combines education, open documentation, and practical
workflow design so that personal knowledge remains portable and inspectable
instead of locked inside private platforms.
